# Unit 6: Prepositions of Place - Lesson Plan

**Target:** Grammar Book 1 students  
**Duration:** 90-120 minutes (adjustable)  
**Topic:** Describing locations using prepositions of place

---

## Learning Outcomes
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- Use prepositions of place correctly (in, on, at, between, next to, etc.)
- Describe locations of objects and places
- Understand addresses and directions in English

---

## Warm-up (10 mins)
**Activity:** "Where's My Phone?"
- Hide an object in the classroom
- Students ask: "Is it under the desk?" "Is it next to the board?"
- Teacher responds with Yes/No
- First student to guess correctly wins

**Purpose:** Activate prior knowledge, get students thinking spatially

---

## Reading: American Museum of Natural History (20 mins)

### Pre-Reading (5 mins)
Discuss with students:
1. What's your favorite museum?
2. Where is it?
3. Why do you like it?

### While Reading (10 mins)
- Students read the text (page 63-64)
- Underline all prepositions they find
- Check vocabulary: gem, skeleton, huge, located

### Post-Reading (5 mins)
**Comprehension questions:**
1. Where is the museum? (New York City, Central Park West between 77th and 81st Street)
2. What's on the first floor? (94-foot whale, Star of India sapphire)
3. Where are the dinosaurs? (Fourth floor)

---

## Grammar Presentation (25 mins)

### Part 1: One-word prepositions (10 mins)
Teach with visuals/gestures:
- **in** (inside something): in the box, in New York
- **on** (surface contact): on the table, on Fifth Avenue
- **at** (specific point): at the door, at 78 Main Street
- **under** (below): under the table
- **behind** (in back of): behind the chair
- **between** (in the middle of two things): between the book and the pen
- **next to** (beside): next to the window
- **near** (close to): near the museum

### Part 2: Multi-word prepositions (8 mins)
- **in front of** ↔ **in back of**
- **across from** (opposite side)
- **on the left** / **on the right** / **in the middle**
- **on the corner of** (intersection)

### Part 3: Special cases (7 mins)
**at/in/on with work, home, school:**
- I'm **at work** / **at home** / **at school**
- I'm **in** school (enrolled as student)

**Addresses:**
- **in** + city/country: in Vietnam, in Hanoi
- **on** + street/floor: on Nguyen Hue Street, on the 3rd floor
- **at** + building number: at 25 Le Loi Street

## Controlled Practice (20 mins)

### Exercise 1: Gap-fill (8 mins)
Use Exercise 2 from textbook (map activity, page 68)
- Students complete sentences using: at, between, next to, on, on the corner of, under

### Exercise 2: Error Correction (7 mins)
Use Exercise 4 from textbook (page 69)
- Find and correct 6 mistakes in conversations

### Quick Check (5 mins)
Teacher draws simple map on board, asks:
- "Where is the bank?" → Students answer using prepositions

---

## Freer Practice (25 mins)

### Activity 1: Classroom Description (10 mins)
**Pair work:**
- Student A describes where 5 objects are in the classroom
- Student B listens and points to each object
- Switch roles

**Example:** "The clock is on the wall above the door."

### Activity 2: Draw My Picture (15 mins)
Use Exercise 8 from textbook (page 71)
- Student A adds items to Picture A (butterfly, glasses, book, star, clock)
- Student A describes locations WITHOUT showing the picture
- Student B draws in Picture B based on description
- Compare pictures

---

## Wrap-up & Homework (10 mins)

### Review Game: "Simon Says with Prepositions"
- Teacher: "Put your pen **on** the desk"
- Students follow commands
- Catch mistakes (e.g., "Put your book **at** the chair" → incorrect!)

### Homework:
1. **Writing task:** Write an invitation to a party (page 72)
   - Must include: location, address, date, time
   - Use at least 5 different prepositions
2. **Vocabulary:** Complete Exercise 1 (page 64) if not done in class

---

## Teacher Notes

**Common Mistakes to Watch:**
- Using "in" instead of "on" for streets: ~~in Main Street~~ → **on Main Street**
- Forgetting "of" after "in front/in back": ~~in front the door~~ → **in front of the door**
- Using cardinal numbers for floors: ~~on three floor~~ → **on the third floor**

**Extension for Advanced Students:**
- Describe route from school to home using prepositions
- Play "20 Questions" with locations (Is it near...? Is it between...?)

**Differentiation:**
- **Struggling students:** Focus on basic prepositions (in, on, at, under, next to)
- **Advanced students:** Add "above," "below," "opposite," "among"
